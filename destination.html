Space is weird. Down is up, and up is down -depending on your frame of reference, of course! Continuum is currently offerring trips to 3 locations.



Take a trip exploring the magnetosphere of Jupiter. If you think the aurora on earth are strong. The solar wind creates a cavity in the planets magnetosphere. It is the largest and most powerful of any planetary magnetosphere in our Solar System, and by volume the largest known continuous structure on the Solar System after the heliosphere.


Visit the curiosity rover on Mars. It originally launced from earth on November 26,2011 and landed in Mars the following year travelling an astonishing 560 million miles. Since then it has been performing tests on the martian atmosphere and soil providing invaluable data to our scientitits. Do not let Curiosity spend another birthday alone!



Working on your tan? Book a trip to Kepler 16b. A planet the size of Saturn, it was the first discovered by the Kepler Space Telescope as the first unambiguous example of a circumbinary planet-a planet orbiting two stars!
